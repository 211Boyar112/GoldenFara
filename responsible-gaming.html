Recognizing Problem Gambling

Warning Signs to Watch For

Problem gambling can affect anyone. Be aware of these signs in yourself or others:

Behavioral Signs

  • Spending more time or money on gambling than intended
  • Chasing losses (trying to win back money lost)
  • Borrowing money to gamble or pay gambling debts
  • Lying about gambling activities or hiding them from others
  • Neglecting work, family, or personal responsibilities
  • Gambling to escape problems or relieve negative emotions

Financial Signs

  • Difficulty paying bills or managing expenses
  • Using savings or essential funds for gambling
  • Maxing out credit cards or taking loans for gambling
  • Selling personal items to get gambling money
  • Frequent requests for financial help from friends/family

Emotional Signs

  • Feeling restless or irritable when not gambling
  • Gambling until all money is gone
  • Experiencing guilt, anxiety, or depression about gambling
  • Failed attempts to control, cut back, or stop gambling
  • Gambling to celebrate wins or cope with losses

Gambling Self-Assessment

Answer these questions honestly to assess your gambling habits:

  • Do you gamble more than you can afford to lose?
  • Do you need to gamble with larger amounts of money to get the same feeling?
  • Have you tried to cut back on gambling but been unsuccessful?
  • Do you feel restless or irritable when trying to cut down on gambling?
  • Do you gamble to escape problems or relieve uncomfortable feelings?
  • After losing money, do you return to try to win it back ("chasing losses")?
  • Do you lie to conceal your gambling from others?
  • Have you jeopardized or lost relationships, jobs, or opportunities due to gambling?
  • Do you rely on others for money to solve financial problems caused by gambling?

If you answered "yes" to any of these questions, you may want to:

  • Use our player protection tools to set limits
  • Contact one of the support organisations listed below
  • Consider taking a break from gambling

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I set deposit limits?

Log into your account, go to "My Account" > "Responsible Gaming" > "Deposit Limits". You can set daily, weekly, or monthly limits. Decreases take effect immediately, while increases may have a 24-hour cooling-off period.

What is self-exclusion and how does it work?

Self-exclusion allows you to exclude yourself from gambling for a minimum of 6 months. During this period, you cannot deposit, wager, or receive promotional communications. To self-exclude, go to "My Account" > "Responsible Gaming" > "Self-Exclusion".

Can I cancel my self-exclusion early?

No, self-exclusion periods cannot be cancelled early. This is to ensure you have sufficient time away from gambling. After the exclusion period ends, there is a 24-hour cooling-off period before you can gamble again.

Are the protection tools reversible?

Most tools can be adjusted, but with cooling-off periods. Decreasing limits takes effect immediately. Increasing limits or ending cooling-off periods typically requires a 24-hour wait. Self-exclusion cannot be reversed early.

What happens if I try to create a new account while self-excluded?

Our systems will detect the attempt and prevent account creation. Any funds deposited will be returned, and winnings will be forfeited. Attempting to circumvent self-exclusion is a violation of our terms and conditions.

How can I get help for someone else's gambling?

Contact GamCare's helpline (0808 8020 133) for advice on helping someone with gambling problems. You can also use our "Third-Party Concern" form by contacting customer support. We can provide guidance on available options.
